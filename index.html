What is a Privacy Impact Assessment?

A Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) is a systematic process for identifying and addressing privacy risks in a project or activity. The Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) recommends that organisations conduct a PIA whenever a new project or activity may have a significant impact on the privacy of individuals.

When should I conduct a PIA?

No PIA required

The project does not involve personal information, or continues existing practices without significant change and carries negligible privacy risk.

Short-form PIA

The project involves personal information with limited risk indicators — for example, a small number of individuals, no sensitive data, and well-understood information flows.

Full PIA

The project involves sensitive information, a large volume of personal data, new technology, data matching, or overseas transfers — or the privacy risks are complex or uncertain.
